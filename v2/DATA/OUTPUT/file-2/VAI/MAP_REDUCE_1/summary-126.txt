 1. A banking organization may use the simple approach or the collateral haircut approach to recognize the risk-mitigating effects of financial collateral. 


2. The simple approach allows a banking organization to apply a risk weight to the portion of an exposure that is secured by the fair value of financial collateral based on the risk weight assigned to the collateral under this subpart. 


3. The collateral haircut approach allows a banking organization to recognize the credit risk mitigation benefits of financial collateral that secures an eligible margin loan, repo-style transaction, or netting set of such transactions. 


4. Under the collateral haircut approach, a banking organization must determine the exposure amount for a netting set of eligible margin loans or repo-style transactions according to a specified formula. 


5. The exposure amount is used to calculate the risk-weighted asset amount for the netting set.
