 1. The proposal enhances capital requirements for large banking organizations to improve their risk sensitivity and comprehensiveness.


2. It replaces the use of internal models with a simpler standardized framework for most parts of the risk-based capital framework.


3. The proposal introduces a new standardized approach for market risk and credit valuation adjustment risk and retains the use of internal models for market risk with an improved models-based measure and an output floor.


4. It maintains the capital rule's dual-requirement structure and applies the same treatment of regulatory capital to all large banking organizations.


5. The proposal expands the application of the supplementary leverage ratio and the countercyclical capital buffer to banking organizations subject to Category IV capital standards.
