 1. The banking regulators propose to revise the capital requirements for large banking organizations and those with significant trading activity. 


2. The proposed revisions aim to improve risk-based capital requirements, reduce complexity, enhance consistency, and facilitate effective supervisory and market assessments. 


3. The proposal includes replacing current requirements that use internal models for credit and operational risk with standardized approaches. 


4. It also replaces current market risk and credit valuation adjustment risk requirements with revised approaches. 


5. The proposed revisions generally align with recent changes to international capital standards issued by the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision.
