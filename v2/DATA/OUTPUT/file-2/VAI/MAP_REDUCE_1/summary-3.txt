 1. The proposal would remove the use of internal models to set credit risk and operational risk capital requirements for banking organizations subject to Category I or II capital standards. 


2. The proposal would continue to allow use of internal models to set market risk capital requirements for portfolios where modeling can be demonstrated to be appropriate. 


3. The proposal's expanded risk-based approach would apply to banking organizations with total assets of $100 billion or more and their subsidiary depository institutions. 


4. Banking organizations with significant trading activities would be subject to market risk capital requirements. 


5. Large banking organizations would be subject to market risk capital requirements regardless of trading activities.
