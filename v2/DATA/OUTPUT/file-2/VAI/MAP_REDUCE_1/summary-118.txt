A 100 percent CCF is applied to certain off-balance-sheet items and other similar transactions, including guarantees, repurchase agreements, credit-enhancing representations and warranties, off-balance sheet securities lending and borrowing transactions, financial standby letters of credit, and forward agreements.


2. The exposure amount for derivative contracts is determined using the standardized approach for counterparty credit risk (SA-CCR).


3. Credit derivatives purchased as credit risk mitigants for exposures that are not market risk covered positions are not subject to a separate counterparty credit risk capital requirement.


4. Equity derivative contracts are treated as equity exposures and risk-weighted under relevant sections, but a banking organization may choose not to hold risk-based capital against the counterparty credit risk of the equity derivative contract.


5. The exposure amount of a netting set is calculated as 1.4 multiplied by the sum of the replacement cost and potential future exposure of the netting set, with certain exceptions for netting sets subject to variation margin agreements, fully paid options, and commercial end-users.
