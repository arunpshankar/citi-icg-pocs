1. A banking organization must assign a 20% risk weight to the portion of an exposure that is conditionally guaranteed by the U.S. government, its central bank, or a U.S. government agency, including the FDIC or the National Credit Union Administration.
2. A zero percent risk weight is assigned to a Paycheck Protection Program covered loan as defined in section 7(a)(36) of the Small Business Act.
3. Risk weights for sovereign exposures are assigned based on the CRC applicable to the sovereign or the sovereign's OECD membership status if there is no CRC applicable to the sovereign, with a 150% risk weight assigned immediately upon determining that an event of sovereign default has occurred.
4. A banking organization must assign a 20% risk weight to an exposure to a GSE that is not an equity exposure or an exposure to a subordinated debt instrument issued by a GSE, and a 150% risk weight to an exposure to a subordinated debt instrument issued by a GSE.
5. Risk weights for exposures to public sector entities (PSES) are assigned based on the CRC that corresponds to the PSE's home country or the OECD membership status of the PSE's home country if there is no CRC applicable to the PSE's
