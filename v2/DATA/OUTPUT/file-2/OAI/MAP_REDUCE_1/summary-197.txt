1. A Board-regulated institution providing credit protection must ensure that the reference exposure of the purchased credit derivative is to the same legal entity and ranks pari passu with, or is junior to, the reference exposure of the credit derivative through which the institution provides credit protection. 
2. If the institution reduces the effective notional principal amount of a credit derivative, it must also reduce the effective notional principal amount of a purchased credit derivative used to offset the credit derivative by the amount of any increase in the mark-to-fair value of the purchased credit derivative. 
3. The institution cannot use purchased credit protection to offset the effective notional principal amount of the related credit derivative if it purchases credit protection through a total return swap and records the net payments received on a credit derivative in net income, but does not record offsetting deterioration in the mark-to-fair value of the credit derivative in net income. 
4. If the institution has more than one repo-style transaction with the same counterparty and has offset the gross value of receivables due from a counterparty under reverse repurchase transactions by the gross value of payables under repurchase transactions due to the same counterparty, certain criteria must be met.
