1. A Board-regulated institution must calculate its regulatory capital ratios, including the ratio of its expanded risk-based approach-adjusted total capital to expanded total risk-weighted assets. This calculation involves deducting any AACL from its total capital and adding any AACL up to 1.25 percent of the institution's total credit risk-weighted assets.
2. The capital conservation buffer is composed solely of common equity tier 1 capital. The maximum payout ratio is the percentage of eligible retained income that a Board-regulated institution can pay out in the form of distributions and discretionary bonus payments during the current calendar quarter.
3. The stress capital buffer requirement for a Board-regulated institution is determined under 12 CFR 225.8 or 238.170, and if the institution has not yet received a stress capital buffer requirement, it is 2.5 percent.
4. A Board-regulated institution's capital conservation buffer is calculated as the lowest of the following ratios: common equity tier 1 capital ratio minus the minimum common equity tier 1 capital ratio requirement, tier 1 capital ratio minus the minimum tier 1 capital ratio requirement, and total capital ratio minus the minimum total capital ratio requirement.
