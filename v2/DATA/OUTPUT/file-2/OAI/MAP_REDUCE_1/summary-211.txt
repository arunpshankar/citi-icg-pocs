1. An FDIC-supervised institution can reduce the effective notional principal amount of a credit derivative by the amount of any reduction in the mark-to-fair value of the credit derivative if the reduction is recognized in common equity tier 1 capital.
2. The institution can also reduce the effective notional principal amount of a credit derivative by the effective notional principal amount of a purchased credit derivative or similar instrument, provided certain conditions are met regarding maturity and reference exposure.
3. If the institution has reduced the effective notional principal amount of a credit derivative, it must also reduce the effective notional principal amount of a purchased credit derivative used to offset the credit derivative by the amount of any increase in the mark-to-fair value of the purchased credit derivative that is recognized in common equity tier 1 capital.
4. The institution may not use purchased credit protection to offset the effective notional principal amount of a related credit derivative if it records net payments received on a credit derivative in net income but does not record offsetting deterioration in the mark-to-fair value of the credit derivative in net income.
