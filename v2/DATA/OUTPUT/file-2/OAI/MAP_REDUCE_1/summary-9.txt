1. A banking organization must have reasonable assurance of repayment and performance for all contractual principal and interest payments on an exposure before it can adjust the principal or negative fair value for credit-related reasons. This includes cases where a distressed restructuring of the exposure has been agreed upon.
2. A credit obligation is considered a defaulted exposure if the obligor is 90 days or more past due or in nonaccrual status, or if the banking organization determines that the obligor is unlikely to pay its credit obligations in full. If an obligor meets these criteria, all exposures that are credit obligations of that obligor are treated as defaulted exposures.
3. The proposal requires banking organizations to continue treating an exposure as a defaulted exposure until it no longer meets the definition or until the obligor meets the definition of investment grade or speculative grade. The definitions of speculative grade and sub-speculative grade would be revised to apply to entities to which the banking organization is exposed through a loan or security.
4. A banking organization would assign a 150 percent risk weight to a defaulted exposure to reflect the increased uncertainty as to the recovery of the remaining carrying value. The risk weight can be applied to the guaranteed or secured portion of a defaulted exposure based on certain requirements.
