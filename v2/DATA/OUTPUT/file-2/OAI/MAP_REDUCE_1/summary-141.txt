Foreign exchange risk refers to potential losses due to changes in foreign exchange rates. A hedge is a position that offsets the price risk of another position. Hybrid instruments share characteristics with both debt and equity instruments. Idiosyncratic risk refers to the risk of loss in a position due to unique risk factors related to the issuer. Lastly, an internal risk management model is a valuation model used by a banking organization's independent risk control unit to report market risks and risk-theoretical profits and losses to senior management.
