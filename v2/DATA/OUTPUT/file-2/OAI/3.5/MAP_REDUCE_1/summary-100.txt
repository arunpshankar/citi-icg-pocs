The proposed changes in asset class-specific risk weights would only have a small impact on banking organizations' portfolio allocations. However, it may lead to changes in lending decisions by large banking organizations, which could affect other lenders in the same markets. The proposal includes requirements for large banking organizations to maintain equity capital funding at a level at least as high as that required by the current standardized approach. The proposal also adjusts the U.S. implementation of Basel III reforms to mitigate potential competitive effects between U.S. banking organizations. Higher capital requirements for trading activities could enhance the resilience of bank-affiliated broker dealers and benefit market liquidity, but it may also reduce incentives for certain market making activities and impair market liquidity. The proposal would also affect large banking organizations through changes in the calculation of regulatory capital, TLAC and LTD requirements, single counter-party credit limits, and method 2 GSIB scores.
