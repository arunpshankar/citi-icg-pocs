The information provided discusses the regulations and requirements for Board-regulated institutions regarding their investments in unconsolidated financial institutions and covered debt instruments. It states that non-significant investments in unconsolidated financial institutions must be assigned the appropriate risk weight. Significant investments in the capital of unconsolidated financial institutions that are not in the form of common stock must be deducted from capital. The deductions are net of associated DTAs and with prior approval, certain investments may not be required to be deducted if they are made for the purpose of providing financial support. The information also mentions the deduction thresholds for certain items from common equity tier 1 capital and the recognition of credit risk mitigation benefits of financial collateral.
