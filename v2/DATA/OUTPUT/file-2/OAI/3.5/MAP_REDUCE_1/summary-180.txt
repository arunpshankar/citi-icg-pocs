(i) National banks or Federal savings associations with total consolidated assets of $50 billion or more must make public disclosures as described in subpart D of this part, except for those subject to subpart E.
(ii) National banks or Federal savings associations subject to subpart E must make public disclosures as described in subpart E.
(iii) Transitions and timing: National banks or Federal savings associations must make any adjustments provided in subpart G for implementing this part. If a bank changes categories or goes from having no category to having a category, it must comply with the requirements of its category, including applicable transition provisions, by the first day of the second quarter following the change.
(iv) Section 3.2 of the regulations includes various additions, revisions, and removals of definitions related to national banks and Federal savings associations.
(v) Category II national banks or Federal savings associations are defined as those with total consolidated assets of $700 billion or more, or $100 billion or more with cross-jurisdictional activity of $75 billion or more. Category III national banks or Federal savings associations are defined as those with total consolidated assets of $250 billion or more, or $100 billion or more but less than $250 billion.
