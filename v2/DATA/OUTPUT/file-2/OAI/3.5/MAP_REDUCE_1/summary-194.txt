(1) Category IV banking organizations include depository institution holding companies and U.S. intermediate holding companies.
(2) State member banks can be considered Category IV if they are subsidiaries of Category IV banking organizations or have total consolidated assets of $100 billion or more.
(3) State member banks remain Category IV until their total consolidated assets fall below $100 billion or they become Category II or III Board-regulated institutions.
(4) Corporate exposure refers to exposures to companies that do not fall under specific categories, such as sovereigns, government-sponsored enterprises, or residential mortgages.
(5) CVA risk-weighted assets are calculated by multiplying the measure for CVA risk by 12.5.
