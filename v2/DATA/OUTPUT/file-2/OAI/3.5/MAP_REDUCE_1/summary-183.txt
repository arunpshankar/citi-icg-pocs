Standardized market risk-weighted assets are calculated by multiplying the standardized measure for market risk by 12.5. Standardized total risk-weighted assets for a market risk national bank or Federal savings association are calculated by subtracting the allowance for loan and lease losses or adjusted allowance for credit losses that is not included in tier 2 capital and any allocated transfer risk reserves. Sub-speculative grade refers to an entity that relies on favorable economic conditions to meet its financial commitments and would likely default if economic conditions deteriorate. Synthetic exposure is an exposure linked to the value of a national bank or Federal savings association's own capital instrument or the capital of an unconsolidated financial institution. Total credit risk-weighted assets are the sum of risk-weighted assets for general credit risk, cleared transactions and default fund contributions, unsettled transactions, and securitization exposures.
