 1. Delta sensitivities are assigned to different buckets based on the type of risk, with different risk weights applied to each bucket. 

2. For equity risk, delta sensitivities are further categorized into 13 buckets based on market capitalization and liquidity, with varying correlation parameters applied within and between buckets. 

3. For commodity risk, delta sensitivities are assigned to 11 buckets based on the commodity type, with specific risk weights and correlation parameters applied within and between buckets. 

4. For foreign exchange risk, buckets are established for each exchange rate between the currency of a market risk covered position and the reporting currency, with a risk weight of 8% applied to all foreign exchange risk. 

5. Cross-bucket correlation parameters are applied to aggregate delta bucket-level risk positions within and across risk classes.
