 - The effective notional amount of an eligible credit derivative subject to adjustment is reduced by 60%. 
- If a guarantee or credit derivative is denominated in a different currency than the hedged exposure, the effective notional amount is further adjusted by a haircut appropriate for the currency mismatch. 
- A [BANKING ORGANIZATION] may recognize the risk-mitigating effects of financial collateral by using the simple approach or the collateral haircut approach. 
- Under the simple approach, the risk weight of the collateralized portion of an exposure may be based on the risk weight assigned to the collateral, subject to certain exceptions. 
- Under the collateral haircut approach, the exposure amount of a netting set of eligible margin loans or repo-style transactions is calculated using a formula that includes haircuts appropriate for the instruments or gold involved.
