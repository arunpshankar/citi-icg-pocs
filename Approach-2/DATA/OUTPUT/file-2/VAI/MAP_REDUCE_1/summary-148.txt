 - A banking organization must apply the look-through approach for any market risk covered position that is an index instrument or a multi-underlying option.
- For listed and well-diversified indices, a banking organization may choose not to apply the look-through approach. In this case, a single sensitivity shall be calculated to the index and assigned to the relevant sector or index bucket.
- For multi-underlying options (including an index option), a banking organization may calculate the vega capital requirement based either on the implied volatility of the option or the implied volatility of options on the underlying constituents.
- For indices, a banking organization must calculate the vega capital requirement with respect to the implied volatility of the multi-underlying options based on the same sector-specific bucket or index bucket used to calculate the delta capital requirement and the curvature capital requirement.
- For non-securitization debt or equity positions, a banking organization may apply the look-through approach for multi-underlying options.
- For an equity position in an investment fund that is a market risk covered position, a banking organization must apply the look-through approach to calculate the standardized measure for market risk.
