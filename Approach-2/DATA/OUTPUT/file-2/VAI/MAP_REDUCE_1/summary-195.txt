 - The market risk-weighted assets are calculated by multiplying the measure for market risk by 12.5.
- The standardized market risk-weighted assets are calculated by multiplying the standardized measure for market risk by 12.5.
- The standardized total risk-weighted assets are the sum of the total risk-weighted assets for general credit risk, cleared transactions and default fund contributions, unsettled transactions, securitization exposures, equity exposures, and market risk-weighted assets (for market risk Board-regulated institutions only), less any amount of the Board-regulated institution's allowance for loan and lease losses or adjusted allowance for credit losses, as applicable, that is not included in tier 2 capital and any amount of "allocated transfer risk reserves."
- The total credit risk-weighted assets are the sum of the total risk-weighted assets for general credit risk, cleared transactions and default fund contributions, unsettled transactions, and securitization exposures.
