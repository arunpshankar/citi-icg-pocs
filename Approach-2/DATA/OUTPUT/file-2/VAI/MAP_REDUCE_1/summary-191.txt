 - The final rule implements the CECL transition provisions in the Dodd-Frank Act (DFA) and makes conforming changes to the risk-based capital rules. 
- The final rule requires banking organizations to record a CECL allowance for all covered assets and exposures. 
- The final rule also provides for a five-year phase-in of the impact of the CECL transition on the supplementary leverage ratio (SLR). 
- Banking organizations will be required to increase their SLR by 75% of their CECL transitional amount in the first year of the transition period, 50% in the second year, and 25% in the third year. 
- The final rule also makes conforming changes to the risk-based capital rules to ensure that the rules are consistent with the CECL transition provisions.
