 - Deductions from regulatory capital are required for certain investments in the capital of unconsolidated financial institutions.
- The amount of the deduction is based on the percentage of the investment to the FDIC-supervised institution's total capital.
- Significant investments in the capital of unconsolidated financial institutions in the form of common stock are subject to a 10% common equity tier 1 capital deduction threshold.
- Significant investments in the capital of unconsolidated financial institutions in the form of common stock are subject to a 15% common equity tier 1 capital deduction threshold.
- If a FDIC-supervised institution does not have a sufficient amount of a specific component of capital to effect the full amount of any deduction from capital required, the FDIC-supervised institution must deduct the shortfall amount from the next higher (that is, more subordinated) component of regulatory capital.
