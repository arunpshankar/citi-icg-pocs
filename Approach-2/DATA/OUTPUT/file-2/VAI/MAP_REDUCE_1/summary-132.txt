 - The risk-weighted asset amount for an equity exposure is the adjusted carrying value of the exposure multiplied by the lowest applicable risk weight.
- Equity exposures to certain entities, such as sovereigns and MDBs, may be assigned a zero percent risk weight.
- Equity exposures to PSEs, Federal Home Loan Banks, and Farmer Mac must be assigned a 20 percent risk weight.
- Certain equity exposures, such as community development investments and investments in small business investment companies, must be assigned a 100 percent risk weight.
- Publicly traded equity exposures and significant investments in the capital of unconsolidated financial institutions must be assigned a 250 percent risk weight.
