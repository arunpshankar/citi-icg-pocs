 - A Board-regulated institution's capital conservation buffer is the lowest of its common equity tier 1, tier 1, or total capital ratios minus the respective minimum capital ratios.
- If a Board-regulated institution's capital ratio is less than or equal to its minimum capital ratio, its capital conservation buffer is zero.
- A global systemically important BHC's leverage buffer is equal to its supplementary leverage ratio minus 3%.
- If a global systemically important BHC's supplementary leverage ratio is less than or equal to 3%, its leverage buffer is zero.
- A Board-regulated institution's maximum payout ratio is determined based on its capital buffer and leverage buffer.
- A global systemically important BHC must use its GSIB surcharge to calculate its maximum payout ratio.
- A Board-regulated institution subject to subpart E must disclose that the holders of its instruments may be fully subordinated to interests held by the U.S. government in the event of a receivership, insolvency, liquidation, or similar proceeding.
