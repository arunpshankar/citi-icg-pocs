 1. The proposal revises risk-based capital requirements for large banking organizations to improve their comprehensiveness and risk sensitivity.


2. Capital requirements for lending activities would be determined by a combination of the credit risk and operational risk frameworks, resulting in a modest increase.


3. Capital requirements for trading activities would be determined by the market risk, CVA risk, and operational risk frameworks, and are estimated to increase substantially.


4. The proposal is expected to increase total risk-weighted assets by 20 percent for holding companies subject to Category I, II, III or IV standards, and by 9 percent for depository institutions subject to Category I, II, III or IV standards.


5. The impact of the proposal varies meaningfully across banking organizations, depending on each banking organization's activities and risk profile.
