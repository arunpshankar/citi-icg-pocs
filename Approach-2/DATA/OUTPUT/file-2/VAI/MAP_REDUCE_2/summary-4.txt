 I. Introduction
A. Overview of the Proposal
   - The final rule implements the Basel III capital framework for bank holding companies and savings and loan holding companies.
   - The final rule implements the TLAC requirements of the Dodd-Frank Act for U.S. global systemically important banking organizations (G-SIBs).
   - The final rule implements the single counterparty credit limits requirements of the Dodd-Frank Act for bank holding companies, savings and loan holding companies, and U.S. branches and agencies of foreign banking organizations.
   - The final rule implements the enhanced prudential standards requirements of the Dodd-Frank Act for foreign banking organizations with total consolidated assets of $100 billion or more and combined U.S. assets of $100 billion or more.
   - The final rule implements the enhanced prudential standards requirements of the Dodd-Frank Act for foreign banking organizations with total consolidated assets of $100 billion or more and combined U.S. assets of less than $100 billion.
B. Use of Internal Models Under the
Proposed Framework
   - The final rule allows banking organizations to use their own internal models to calculate risk-weighted assets for credit risk and market risk, subject to certain conditions.

II. Scope of Application
   - The final rule applies to bank holding companies and savings and loan holding companies with total consolidated assets of $10 billion or more.
   - The final rule also applies to U.S. branches and agencies of foreign banking organizations with total consolidated assets of $10 billion or more and combined U.S. assets of $100 billion or more.

III. Proposed Changes to the Capital Rule
A. Calculation of Capital Ratios and
Application of Buffer Requirements
   - The final rule increases the minimum common equity tier 1 capital ratio from 4.5% to 4.5%.
   - The final rule increases the minimum total capital ratio from 8% to 8.5%.
   - The final rule introduces a new capital conservation buffer of 2.5%.
B. Definition of Capital
   - The final rule revises the definition of “qualifying residential mortgage” to conform to the definition used by the Federal Reserve Board and the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency.
   - The final rule implements the standardized approach for calculating risk-weighted assets for exposures to securitizations and revises the framework for calculating risk-weighted assets for exposures to certain types of derivatives.
C. Credit Risk
   - The final rule revises the market risk capital requirements to incorporate the Fundamental Review of the Trading Book.
   - The final rule revises the internal models approach for calculating risk-weighted assets for credit risk to align with the Basel III framework.
   - The final rule revises the CVA risk-based capital requirements to align with the Basel III framework.
D. Securitization Framework
   - The final rule makes certain technical changes to the risk-based capital framework, including revising the definition of “small business loan” and the treatment of exposures to sovereign entities.
E. Equity Exposures
   - The final rule provides for a three-year transition period for certain provisions of the final rule.
F. Operational Risk
   - The final rule requires banking organizations to use the standardized approach for calculating risk-weighted assets for operational risk.
G. Disclosure Requirements
   - The final rule requires banking organizations to disclose their risk-weighted assets and capital ratios on a quarterly basis.
H. Market Risk
   - The final rule requires banking organizations to disclose their market risk capital requirements on a quarterly basis.
I. Credit Valuation Adjustment Risk
   - The final rule requires banking organizations to disclose their CVA risk capital requirements on a quarterly basis.

IV. Transition Provisions
A. Transitions for Expanded Total Risk-Weighted Assets
   - The final rule provides for a three-year transition period for certain provisions of the final rule.
B. AOCI Regulatory Capital Adjustments
   - The final rule provides for a three-year transition period for certain provisions of the final rule.
