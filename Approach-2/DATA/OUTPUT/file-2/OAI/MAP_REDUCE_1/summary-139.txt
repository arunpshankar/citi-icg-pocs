A banking organization is no longer subject to certain regulations if it is no longer a depository institution holding company that is a global systemically important BHC, a Category II, III, or IV Board-regulated institution, or a U.S. intermediate holding company that is a Category II, III, or IV Board-regulated institution. This is applicable if the organization provides notice to the agency and meets the standard in paragraph (b)(2) for four consecutive quarters.
2. The agency can exclude a banking organization from certain regulations if it determines that the exclusion is appropriate based on the level of market risk or level of CVA risk of the organization and is consistent with safe and sound banking practices.
3. A banking organization that does not have four quarters of aggregate data on trading assets and trading liabilities must calculate the average by averaging as much data as it has available, unless the agency notifies the organization in writing to use an alternative method.
4. The agency can apply certain regulations to any banking organization if it deems it necessary or appropriate because of the level of market risk or CVA risk of the organization or to ensure safe and sound banking practices.
