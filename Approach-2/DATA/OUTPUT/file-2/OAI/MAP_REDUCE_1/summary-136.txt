1. The banking organization's management can disclose all required information in one place on their public website or across multiple public financial or regulatory reports. If disclosures are not centralized, a summary table indicating the locations of all disclosures must be provided.
2. The banking organization must have a formal disclosure policy approved by the board of directors, addressing its approach for determining disclosures, internal controls, and disclosure controls and procedures. The board and senior management are responsible for maintaining an effective internal control structure over financial reporting.
3. If the banking organization concludes that specific commercial or financial information would be exempt from disclosure under the Freedom of Information Act, it is not required to disclose that information. However, more general information about the subject matter must be disclosed, along with the reason for non-disclosure.
4. The banking organization must make public disclosures described in Tables 1 through 15 of this section, available for each of the last twelve quarters or a shorter period if the organization recently became subject to subpart E of this part.
5. The banking organization must describe its risk management objectives and policies, including strategies and processes, the structure and organization of the relevant risk management function, risk reporting and/or measurement systems, and policies for hedging and/or mitigating risk.
