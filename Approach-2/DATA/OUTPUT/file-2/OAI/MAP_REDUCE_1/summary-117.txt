1. A banking organization must assign a 2% or 4% risk weight to an exposure to a QCCP arising from the organization posting cash collateral to the QCCP in connection with a cleared transaction, depending on the requirements met. A 150% risk weight must be assigned to a corporate exposure that is an exposure to a subordinated debt instrument or a covered debt instrument.
2. A 100% risk weight must be assigned to a corporate exposure for the purpose of acquiring or financing equipment or physical commodities where repayment is dependent on the physical assets being financed or acquired, or a project finance operational phase exposure. A 150% risk weight must be assigned to any exposure that is a defaulted exposure.
3. A banking organization must assign a 0% risk weight to cash owned and held in all offices of the organization or in transit, to gold bullion held in the organization's own vaults or held in another depository institution's vaults on an allocated basis, to the extent the gold bullion assets are offset by gold bullion liabilities, and to exposures that arise from the settlement of cash transactions with a central counterparty where there is no assumption of ongoing counterparty credit risk by the central counterparty after settlement of the trade and associated default fund
