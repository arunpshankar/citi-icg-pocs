1. A banking organization must apply a 100 percent Credit Conversion Factor (CCF) to off-balance-sheet items such as guarantees, repurchase agreements, credit-enhancing representations, securities lending and borrowing transactions, financial standby letters of credit, and forward agreements.
2. The exposure amount for derivative contracts must be determined using the standardized approach for counterparty credit risk (SA-CCR), and can be reduced by the credit valuation adjustment recognized in the balance sheet valuation of any derivative contracts in the netting set.
3. Definitions for terms such as end date, start date, and hedging set are provided, with the latter referring to all contracts within a netting set that reference the same currency, risk factor, or commodity category.
4. Banking organizations that purchase credit derivatives as a credit risk mitigant for an exposure that is not a market risk covered position are not required to calculate a separate counterparty credit risk capital requirement, provided they do so consistently for all such credit derivatives.
