1. A national bank or Federal savings association's consolidated assets are calculated based on its total consolidated assets, as reported on the Call Report, for the most recent quarter or average of the most recent quarters. The bank or association must have at least one of the following: total nonbank assets, off-balance sheet exposure, or weighted short-term wholesale funding, each equal to $75 billion or more.
2. A bank or association continues to be a Category III entity until it has less than $250 billion in total consolidated assets, less than $75 billion in total nonbank assets, less than $75 billion in weighted short-term wholesale funding, and less than $75 billion in off-balance sheet exposure for each of the four most recent calendar quarters.
3. A Category IV national bank or Federal savings association is not a Category II or III entity and either is a subsidiary of a Category IV banking organization or has total consolidated assets of $100 billion or more, calculated based on the average of total consolidated assets for the four most recent calendar quarters.
4. A bank or association continues to be a Category IV entity until it has less than $100 billion in total consolidated assets for each of the four most recent calendar quarters or is a Category II or III entity.
