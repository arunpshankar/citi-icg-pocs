1. Banking organizations are required to submit a complete history of operational losses at and above the institution's established collection threshold(s), including a loss event of $20,000 or more on a net basis in its capital calculation. 
2. Insurance recoveries are to be excluded from the "Recovery Amount ($USD))" item in Schedule E.1, and a new "Insurance Recovery Amount ($USD))" item is proposed to be added to avoid double counting. 
3. Banking organizations subject to the advanced approaches framework are required to report probability of default (PD), loss given default (LGD), expected loss given default (ELGD), and exposure at default (EAD) values on various schedules, as specified in the Board's capital rule. 
4. The FR Y-15 quarterly report collects systemic risk data from U.S. bank holding companies and covered savings and loan holding companies with total consolidated assets of $100 billion or more, and is used to monitor the systemic risk profile of these institutions. 
5. The Regulatory Flexibility Act requires an agency to consider whether the rule it proposes will have a significant economic impact on a substantial number of small entities, and the Board believes that this proposed rule will not have such an impact.
