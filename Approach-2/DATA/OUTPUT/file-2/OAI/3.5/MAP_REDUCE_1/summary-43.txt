The information provided discusses various disclosure requirements for banking organizations related to risk management objectives and policies, credit risk mitigation, securitization, credit quality of assets, counterparty credit risk, CVA risk, operational risk, and market risk. The disclosures include information on risk management frameworks, processes, and guidelines, as well as the impact of credit ratings and support on collateral requirements. The proposal also requires disclosures on the scope of past due and defaulted exposures, loan modifications, and regulatory capital instruments. The market risk section highlights the adoption of risk-based capital standards for market risk and subsequent modifications to address deficiencies and capture tail risks. The Basel Committee conducted a review of the market risk capital framework to further address structural shortcomings.
