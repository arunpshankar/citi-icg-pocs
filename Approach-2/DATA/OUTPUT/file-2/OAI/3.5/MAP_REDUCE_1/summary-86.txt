1. Only exposures involving tranching of credit risk qualify as securitization exposures.
2. Mortgage-backed pass-through securities without tranching of credit risk do not meet the definition of a securitization exposure.
3. OTC derivative contracts have both market value and credit risk associated with them.
4. The proposed capital requirements for credit valuation adjustment (CVA) risk would apply to large banking organizations subject to Category I, II, III, or IV standards.
5. CVA risk covered positions are defined as derivative contracts that are not cleared transactions, and certain credit risk mitigants can be excluded from the calculation of CVA risk.
