The expanded risk-based framework would result in higher risk-weighted assets for large banking organizations, making it the binding risk-based approach. This would lead to capital requirements becoming more sensitive to the specific risks of these organizations. The risk weights for credit risk exposures would be more detailed under the expanded framework. The inclusion of operational and CVA risk components ensures better risk management. The proposal would increase the common equity tier 1 capital requirement for large holding companies by around 16 percent. While the proposal may slightly reduce bank lending, it would enhance the resilience of the financial system and support economic stability. The changes in required capital may also influence portfolio allocation decisions for different asset classes.
