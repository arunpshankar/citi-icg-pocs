2. If a banking organization cannot identify the underlying positions held by an investment fund on a quarterly basis, it must exclude those equity positions from the calculation of IMAGA.
3. A banking organization can include term repo-style transactions in market risk if they are marked to market and the organization captures the market price risk and issuer-default risk.
4. Internal risk transfers can be recognized as risk mitigation benefits if they meet certain criteria for credit risk and interest rate risk.
5. A banking organization can hedge CVA risk through internal CVA hedges executed with its trading desk, subject to certain requirements.
