- Regulatory retail exposure refers to a retail exposure that meets specific criteria, including the type of credit, aggregate limit, and granularity limit.
- Retail exposure is an exposure to a natural person or small or medium-sized entity (SME) that meets certain criteria.
- Senior securitization exposure refers to a securitization exposure that has a first-priority claim on the cash flows from the underlying exposures.
- SME is defined as an entity with annual revenues or sales of $50 million or less.
- Transactor exposure is a regulatory retail exposure that has been fully repaid or has had no drawdown over the previous 12 months.
