1. Default derivatives allow exclusion of low-risk assets from capital calculation.
2. Purchased nth-to-default credit derivatives cannot be recognized as credit risk mitigants.
3. Guarantees and credit derivatives are risk-weighted based on the portion of the reference exposure covered.
4. Purchased credit derivatives can be recognized as credit risk mitigants, but if not, exposure amount must be determined separately.
5. Risk weight for securitization exposure is calculated based on attachment and detachment points, with different weights for different types of securitization.
