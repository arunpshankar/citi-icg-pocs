(i) The countercyclical capital buffer is an extension of the capital conservation buffer for Board-regulated institutions.
(ii) The countercyclical capital buffer amount is determined by calculating the weighted average of the buffer amounts established for the jurisdictions where the institution's credit exposures are located.
(iii) The weight assigned to a jurisdiction's buffer amount is based on the institution's private sector credit exposures in that jurisdiction compared to its total private sector credit exposures.
(iv) The location of a credit exposure is determined by the jurisdiction where the borrower or protection provider is located.
(v) The countercyclical capital buffer amount for credit exposures in the United States can be adjusted between zero percent and 2.5 percent of risk-weighted assets based on macroeconomic and financial indicators of systemic risk.
